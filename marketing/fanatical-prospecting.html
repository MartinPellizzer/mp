3 core laws of prospecting:

- the universal law of need

- the 30-day rule

- the law of replacement

The universal law of need

When your pipeline is empty, you became needy and desperate.

The more you need something, the less likely you will get it.

When all your hopes rests on few prospects, the probability of failure increases exponentially.

The 30-day rule

This rule is for B2B and high-end B2C.

The prospectinge you do in thiis 30-day period will pay off for the next 90 days.

Ignore it at your peril.

The law of replacement

Constantly push new opportunities into the pipeline to replace those that will naturally fall out.

Do it at a rate that matches or exceeds your closing ratio (ie. 10% closing rate - add 10 new prospects).

The anatomy of a sales slump

99% of sales slumps is because of lack of prospecting.

- you stop prospecting (30-day rule)

- you pipeline stalls (law of replacement)

- you stop closing deals

- you lose confidence

- you feel like a loser

- you lose motivation about prospecting

- you keep calling dead-end prospects

- you stall the pipe even more

- you look for silver bullets (law of need)

- you enter the black hole of misery

The first rule of sales slumps

Commit to daily prospecting. Commit to daily goals.

Consistency beats talent and techniques.
